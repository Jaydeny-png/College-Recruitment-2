Cost and Financial Aid

Obtain an exceptional education at an affordable price at Rutgers University. We are the only New Jersey research university included in Kiplinger's 100 Best Values in American public colleges and universities.

Undergraduate Tuition & Fees

  • In-state: $17,239
  • Out-of-state: $36,001

Graduate Tuition & Fees

  • In-state: $22,348
  • Out-of-state: $36,244

Additional Costs

  • Books and supplies: $1,391
  • Room and board (on-campus): $14,715
  • Room and board (off-campus): $18,414

Total Cost of Attendance

  • On-campus: $56,479
  • Off-campus: $67,566

1. Grants and Scholarships

  • Federal Pell Grant: For students with significant financial need.
  • New Jersey Tuition Aid Grant (TAG): State-funded for NJ residents.
  • Rutgers Scholarships: Merit-based awards like the Presidential Scholarship.

2. Federal Loans

  • Subsidized Loans: Need-based, no interest while in school.
  • Unsubsidized Loans: Available to all, interest accrues while in school.
  • PLUS Loans: For parents of undergraduates or graduate students.

3. Work-Study Programs

Part-time employment for students with financial need.

4. Institutional Aid

  • Scarlet Promise Grants: For NJ residents with high financial need.
  • Emergency Financial Assistance: Short-term aid for sudden financial difficulties.

5. Aid for Specific Groups

  • Veterans Benefits: Special resources like the Post-9/11 GI Bill.
  • International Student Aid: Scholarships for international students.

6. Application Process

Apply for aid through FAFSA or the NJ Alternative Financial Aid Application for NJ Dreamers.

1. Tuition Payment Plan (RUTPP)

Divide tuition into multiple installments for the academic year or by semester.

2. Payment Deadlines

Payments are due by the first day of classes to avoid late fees.

3. Graduate Payroll Deduction Plan

Graduate assistants can use a nine-payment deduction plan for fees.

For more details, visit the Rutgers Student Accounting page.

Our Alumni

Rutgers University has a strong history of producing successful alumni across various industries.

Selman Waksman

Selman Waksman, a Rutgers alumnus and Nobel Prize winner, discovered **streptomycin**, the first antibiotic for tuberculosis, at Rutgers in 1943. His work transformed medicine and is honored through the **Waksman Institute of Microbiology** at the university.

Career Outcomes

Over 90% of Rutgers graduates secure employment or pursue further education within six months of graduating. Alumni work at top companies like Google, Amazon, and Johnson & Johnson.

Industry Partnerships and Internships

Rutgers has strong connections with companies like Johnson & Johnson and Prudential, offering extensive internship opportunities. Over 75% of students complete internships or co-ops, boosting their career prospects.
